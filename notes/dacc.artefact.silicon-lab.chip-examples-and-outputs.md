## Description

This piece shows the concrete, tangible things a visitor can actually make or understand through [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.chip-design-workstation]] — turning an abstract "you can design a chip" pitch into a set of real objects and outcomes a visitor can see, and in some cases hold. Target examples include a USB text-string device, an LED blinking-pattern chip, a display-image chip, an audio/synthesizer chip, chip art with images embedded in silicon, and winning d/acc chip designs displayed as standalone art objects.

A visitor should leave this display with a concrete answer to "so what would I actually end up with?" before or after they try the workstation themselves.

## Curatorial notes

Open items:

- confirm which example chips exist as physical objects versus renderings or simulations — this materially changes what can be displayed and how;
- decide display format per example: sealed case, live screen demo, or supervised hands-on handling;
- if any example chips are handled by visitors, handling/wear/replacement policy needs to be defined.

## Ideas

- A rotating "chip of the month" slot could keep this display fresh and give [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.workshops-and-programmes]] hackathon winners a reason to keep entering.
- Consider letting visitors vote on a favourite design among recent outputs — low-cost way to add a sense of live community activity to what could otherwise be a static case.
- If most examples turn out to be renderings rather than physical objects, it may be worth leaning into a large shared screen format rather than a traditional display case.
