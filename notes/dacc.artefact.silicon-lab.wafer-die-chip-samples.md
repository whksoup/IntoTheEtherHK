## Description

The physical proof that chip design becomes material: real wafers, dies, packaged chips, and boards, shown up close via microscope or macro-imaging in a display case. Where [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.chip-fabrication-process]] tells the story of fabrication and [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.openpdk-tooling]] shows the software behind it, this piece is the object itself — the thing a visitor can put their face close to and see the actual structure of a chip.

The takeaway for a visitor is simple but important: everything else in the Silicon Lab is not hypothetical. This is what a chip actually looks like, at every stage from raw wafer to finished, packaged part.

## Curatorial notes

Open items — these are collections-management questions and should be resolved before final design:

- full object list with dimensions;
- fragility assessment and insurance requirements per object;
- whether any objects can be touched by visitors, and under what supervision;
- lighting and magnification requirements for the case/display.

## Ideas

- If originals are too fragile or valuable to allow touching, consider tactile replicas alongside the real objects so visitors who want a hands-on experience aren't left out.
- A digital microscope feed (live camera on a real sample, shown on a nearby screen) could let visitors zoom into detail without any handling risk at all — worth costing against a fixed optical microscope.
