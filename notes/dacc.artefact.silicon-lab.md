## Description

The **Silicon Lab** is a themed installation within [[dacc.concept.in-the-ether-3]] that walks visitors through the full journey of a computer chip — from raw material, through design, fabrication, and packaging, to real-world use — and uses that journey to make a larger point: that silicon, historically one of the most closed and capital-intensive technologies in the world, can be opened up. The lab pairs physical objects (quartz, wafers, dies, packaged chips) with an interactive design workstation, so a visitor doesn't just look at chips but leaves having designed one, even in a small symbolic way.

This note is the parent index for the Silicon Lab. It does not itself hold detailed content — each physical or experiential component of the lab is documented as its own sub-artefact, listed below. Read this note first to understand how the pieces fit together; read the sub-artefacts for the specifics a docent, fabricator, or interpretive writer would need.

Sub-artefacts:

- [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.chip-design-workstation]] — the hands-on interface where visitors design or customise their own chip.
- [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.chip-examples-and-outputs]] — the finished visitor-output types (USB device, LED chip, display chip, audio chip, chip art) on display.
- [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.openpdk-tooling]] — the open-source design toolchain (OpenPDK) behind the workstation, shown as its own exhibit.
- [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.wafer-die-chip-samples]] — physical wafers, dies, and packaged chips as tangible proof of the process.
- [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.workshops-and-programmes]] — the scheduled and drop-in programming layered onto the lab.

- [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.modular-lab-furniture]] — the modular furniture/infrastructure system the whole lab sits on.
