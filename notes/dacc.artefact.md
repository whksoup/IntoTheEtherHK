## Description

An **artefact** is the atomic unit of exhibition content: a single document, physical object, image, or point of interest that a visitor can encounter and that warrants its own dedicated interpretation. Every artefact note is a self-contained record of one "thing" in the exhibition — not the exhibition itself, not a whole gallery, but the individual item a visitor stops in front of, reads a label for, or is guided toward.

An artefact note exists to answer, for that one item: *what is this, why is it here, and what should a visitor take away from it?* Any agent working from this note should treat each artefact as a discrete, citable unit that can be referenced, linked, sequenced into a route, or pulled into label copy, audio guide scripts, wall text, or digital interactives — always with the assumption that the eventual audience is **the museum-going public**, not curators or researchers. Content written for an artefact should be accurate and well-sourced, but expressed in clear, accessible, engaging language suited to a general visitor with no specialist background, unless a note explicitly states otherwise.

## Linked concepts

Use this section to connect the artefact to the wider knowledge graph of the exhibition — the ideas, themes, people, events, or other artefacts that give it meaning. This is where an agent should record *why this artefact matters in relation to other things*, e.g. a shared theme with another artefact, a historical figure it belongs to, a technique or movement it exemplifies, or a narrative thread running through the exhibition.

Link liberally using Dendron `[[wikilinks]]` to other artefact, theme, or concept notes. An agent populating this section should think of it as building the associative context a docent would draw on when connecting one object to the bigger story — not just isolated trivia about the artefact itself.

## Section

Records which physical or thematic **section** of the exhibition this artefact belongs to (i.e. which gallery, room, zone, or chapter of the visitor's journey). This should link to the relevant Section note where one exists. An agent should use this field to understand sequencing and grouping — which other artefacts a visitor will likely encounter alongside this one, and what stage of the overall narrative arc this artefact sits within.

## Curatorial notes

Internal, curator-facing content: provenance, sourcing, scholarly disputes, interpretive decisions, sensitivities, and reasoning behind how the artefact is being presented (or deliberately *not* presented) to the public. This section is not meant for visitor-facing output — it is context an agent should draw on to make informed decisions and avoid factual or ethical missteps, but should not surface verbatim in public-facing copy unless explicitly asked to.

## Ideas

A working space for unresolved or exploratory material: possible interpretive angles, open questions, alternative framings, potential connections not yet confirmed, or draft phrasing to workshop. Content here is provisional. An agent should feel free to draft and propose here, but should not treat anything in this section as settled or ready for public use unless promoted into Description, Linked concepts, or elsewhere.
