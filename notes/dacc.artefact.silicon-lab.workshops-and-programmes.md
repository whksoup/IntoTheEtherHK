## Description

The programming layer that sits on top of the physical Silicon Lab, supporting both passive exhibition visitors and visitors who want a deeper, scheduled learning moment. Formats designed for:

- **Open exhibition flow** — casual visitors encounter the lab during normal circulation and grasp the idea quickly, with no booking required.
- **Structured chip-design workshop** — a scheduled, once-a-week class where participants customise a chip from templates in more depth than the walk-up workstation allows.
- **Silicon tapeout workshop** — a guided session walking through design → MPW → fabrication → mailed chip, i.e. the full real pipeline.
- **Hackathon / design competition** — prizes and display for the most interesting d/acc chip designs, feeding [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.chip-examples-and-outputs]].
- **Artist residency / chip art** — artists design silicon works that can be folded into the exhibit or a future version of it.
- **Themed days** — e.g. Silicon Day, Clean Air, Privacy, Biotech.
- **Schools / university sessions** — an education/upskilling angle, likely needing a class-style layout and facilitator station.
- **Online / community extension** — chip-design classes or continuation beyond the physical visit.

## Curatorial notes

Spatial requirements to plan for:

- flexible table/workbench configuration that can shift between open-flow and class layouts;
- facilitator sightlines during structured sessions;
- projector/screen or large shared display for group instruction;
- queue, registration, and transition space between programme formats;
- storage for workshop kits;
- a clear physical/operational separation between public walk-up use and booked sessions, so one doesn't disrupt the other.

## Ideas

- The tapeout workshop (full design → MPW → fabrication → mailed chip pipeline) is the most resource-intensive format listed — worth treating as a flagship, lower-frequency offering rather than a regular fixture.
- Themed days could be a good hook for local press/community partners per theme (e.g. a Biotech day partnering with a local bio-hacking group) — distinct from the core silicon narrative but keeps the space active.
- Online/community extension is the one format that outlives the physical exhibition — worth scoping early since it may need separate platform/hosting decisions.
