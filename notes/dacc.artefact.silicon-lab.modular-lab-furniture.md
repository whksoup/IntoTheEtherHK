## Description

The physical shell the whole Silicon Lab sits on: benches, workstations, screen stands, signage and interpretive panels, and plinths/cases for wafers, boards, and chips. It's designed to be modular and portable, so the installation can travel to or be reconfigured for future venues rather than being a one-off build, and to switch between **workshop mode** and **open exhibition mode** without a full rebuild.

A visitor rarely notices furniture as its own artefact, but it's what makes every other piece in the lab — [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.chip-design-workstation]], [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.wafer-die-chip-samples]], [[dacc.artefact.silicon-lab.workshops-and-programmes]] — physically usable, accessible, and safe.

## Curatorial notes

Design requirements:

- lab benches / workstations;
- screen stands / monitor arms;
- signage and interpretive panels;
- plinths / cases for wafers, boards, and chips;
- a defined workshop-mode vs. open-exhibition-mode reconfiguration process;
- cable management, power, ventilation, access, and supervision;
- pack-down, travel, and reconfiguration logic for future venues.

This is primarily a production/infrastructure note rather than visitor-facing content — it exists to keep the physical build honest against the needs of every other sub-artefact in the lab, not to be read or interpreted by a visitor directly.

## Ideas

- Since portability is an explicit goal, worth defining a "kit of parts" early (a small number of standardised modules that combine differently) rather than bespoke furniture per zone — makes both this venue's build and any future venue's rebuild cheaper.
- Given the d/acc framing, there could be a case for the furniture itself being built from an openly documented/open-hardware design — a small extra layer of thematic consistency, if budget and timeline allow.
