## Description

GrapheneOS is an independent, open-source mobile operating system
focused on making smartphones substantially more resistant to compromise
through deep systems engineering rather than through surveillance or
centralized control. Originally founded by Daniel Micay in 2014, the
project began as an effort to strengthen Android's security foundations
before evolving into a comprehensive rethinking of Android hardening.

Unlike many privacy-focused projects that primarily seek to reduce data
collection, GrapheneOS treats privacy as an emergent property of robust
security. Its work emphasizes memory safety mitigations, exploit
prevention, application sandboxing, permission isolation, hardened
allocators, verified boot, and secure hardware integration. Many of its
innovations later influenced or were incorporated into the Android Open
Source Project, demonstrating how defensive research can become public
infrastructure.

Following a split from its former commercial sponsor, the project
re-established itself as an independent community-led effort and formed
the GrapheneOS Foundation in 2023 to provide governance while preserving
technical independence.

Viewed as an artefact, GrapheneOS represents the acceleration of
defensive technology: increasing computational capability is directed
toward making exploitation progressively more expensive, less scalable,
and less viable.

## Curatorial notes

GrapheneOS embodies a recurring theme in Vitalik Buterin's
techno-optimism: accelerating technology can strengthen society's
defensive capacity rather than expand surveillance or coercion. Its
influence on upstream Android illustrates how open-source
experimentation can raise the security baseline for millions of users.
Its institutional history also demonstrates the importance of governance
structures that preserve technical independence over commercial
incentives.
