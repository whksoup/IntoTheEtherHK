## Description

If feasible, [[dacc.people.vitalik-buterin]] performs or launches a chip-design/tapeout moment in person, giving the Silicon Lab a symbolic, public proof point: if he can design a chip on this system, so can you. The moment is designed to work either as a live keynote/demo, or as a recorded and documented version if a live appearance isn't feasible on the day.

For a visitor, this piece (when present) is less about the chip itself and more about credibility and momentum — a recognisable figure closely associated with [[dacc.concept.d-acc]] treating the workstation as something worth using himself.

## Curatorial notes

Explicit design constraint from the source brief: **do not make the entire Silicon Lab dependent on this being live.** The lab must be designed so this moment can be layered in if it happens, without the rest of the exhibit's success depending on it.

Open items:

- confirm whether a live keynote/demo is realistic given Vitalik's schedule and the event timeline;
- if live isn't feasible, agree on what the recorded/documented fallback actually consists of (video loop? photo + design artefact on display? both?);
- decide what "symbolic representation" looks like in the lab if fabrication timing shifts and no chip is physically ready in time — e.g. a placeholder case, a countdown, or a design-only display.
- coordinate directly with whoever owns [[dacc.artefact.vitalik-statement]] so the two moments aren't developed in isolation and don't contradict each other in tone or framing.

## Ideas

- A "his design is being fabricated right now" framing (even if the physical chip isn't ready for the exhibition dates) could be more honest and still compelling than waiting to display only a finished object.
- If live isn't possible, a short recorded piece filmed specifically for this exhibit (rather than repurposed generic footage) would likely land better with visitors — worth flagging early given lead time for filming/editing.
