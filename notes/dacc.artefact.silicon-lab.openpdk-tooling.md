## Description

Where most of the Silicon Lab treats a chip as a physical object, this piece makes the case that **open silicon is also, maybe primarily, an open software stack**. It shows [[dacc.concept.openpdk|openpdk]] — open-source chip design tooling — through screenshots or a live demo, with diagrams explaining the open toolchain and how it connects to real fabrication pathways such as [[dacc.concept.tiny-tapeout]], [[dacc.concept.multi-project-wafer]] runs, and the GF180 process at GlobalFoundries.

For a visitor, the takeaway is that the barrier to designing a real chip has dropped further than most people assume — this isn't a proprietary black box, it's a toolchain an individual can, in principle, learn and use.
